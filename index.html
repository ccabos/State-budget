Portfolio view: countries by expenditure category

Expenditure items are normalized into standard categories across countries. Each dot is one budget year; lines connect consecutive years of the same country. Per-capita values are converted to euros at the year's average exchange rate; GDP shares use each country's nominal GDP in its own currency. A category a country does not report separately counts as zero. "Federal / central budgets" shows each government's own budget (California is a US state budget); "General government" combines federal/central, state, local, and social-insurance spending (COFOG basis) — the fairer cross-country comparison, since e.g. German pensions or US Social Security sit in different layers. The United Kingdom and China appear only there: UK figures already cover all levels of government, and China's national general public budget consolidates central and local governments (it excludes land-sales funds and social insurance funds, so its totals are not fully COFOG-comparable). General-government function splits are aligned with curated series from Our World in Data (OECD COFOG, SIPRI; CC BY 4.0); totals come from national statistics.
